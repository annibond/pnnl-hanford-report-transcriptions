== Important Names & Locations to Hanford

[cols="1,1,1"]
|===
^|*Name* ^|*Location* ^|*Purpose*

|Battelle Memorial Institute
|Columbus, OH (primary)
|nonprofit research and development organization that contributed to the Manhattan Project

|Green Glow Program
|Richland, WA
|The Green Glow Diffusion Program at the Hanford site was a series of atmospheric field diffusion experiments conducted in 1959 to test techniques for tracking airborne materials.

|Hanford Nuclear Reservation
|Richland, WA
|site for nuclear testing research and plutonium production during World War II and the Cold War, part of the Manhattan Project

|Liquid-Metal Fast-Breeder Reactor (LMFBR)
|United States, as well as Canada, China, France, India, Japan, Russia, and United Kingdom
|the Experimental Breeder Reactor II (EBR-II) and Fermi 1 in the United States, a three loop system in which liquid sodium cool with an intermediate loop containing non-radioactive sodium transfer heat to a steam generator

|Manhattan Project
|United States, United Kingdom, and Canada
|research and development program undertaken during World War II to produce nuclear weapons on the side of the Allies, directed by United States Major General Leslie Groves

|National Ambient Air Quality Standard (NAAQS)
|Washington D.C.
|regulations set by the United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to protect public health from carbon monoxide, lead, nitrogen dioxide, ozone, particulate matter, and sulfure dioxide

|Naval Arctic Research Laboratory
|Pt. Barrow, AK
|site for nuclear testing on nearby Amchitka Island, which hosted Project Long Shot (1965) and Project Milrow (1969)

|Pacific Northwest Laboratories
|Richland, WA
|also known as the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL), managed by the United States DoE, distinct from Hanford as an employer

|Quillayute Air Reference Station
|Quillayute, WA
|former military air station and current air sampling and monitoring station, named for the federally recognized Native American people, pronounced KWIL-yoot 

|United States Department of Energy
|Washington D.C.
|department of the federal government that oversees national energy policy, the development of nuclear power, and the military's weapons program for the United States
