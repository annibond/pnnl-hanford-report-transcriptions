.Glossary of Terms

[cols="1,1"]
|===
^|*Term* ^|*Definition*

|alpha-emitter
|higher energy, heavier particles with shorter range, more capable of killing or destroying a target

|anion
|negatively charged ion

|assay
|investigative procedure to measure a specific substance or compound

|anthropogenic
|caused or produced by humans

|Avogadro's number
|6.022x10^23^

|beta decay
|radioactive decay in which an unstable atomic nucleus transforms by emitting a beta particle, which is either an electron or a positron

|beta-emitter
|less intense, lighter particles with longer range, more capable of harming surrounding environment

|burn-up
|amount of nuclear fuel consumed in a reactor

|cation
|positively charged ion

|CIE/Q
|relevant data points include nucleus, nuclear energy, nuclear fission, nuclear fusion, and nuclear radiation

|cosmogenic
|produced by cosmic rays

|counts per minute (cpm)
|measurement of the rate of events detected by a radiation monitoring instrument, such as a Geiger counter

|crosswind
|wind blowing in a direction not parallel to a course

|diurnal
|daily

|disintegrations per minute (dpm)
|a unit of measurement for radioactivity

|disintegration ratio rate
|the rate of disintegration and the ratio of the decayed portion to the total

|eddy energy
|kinetic energy associated with the swirling, turbulent motions a fluid (e.g., ocean, air)

|electrical field strength (E/Q)
|a physical field that surrounds electrically charged particles such as electrons

|electrodeposition
|electrochemical process that uses an electric current to deposit a thin layer of a material, such as metal, onto a substrate

|environ
|surrounding area or conditions that may be affected by radiation or nuclear processes

|first order kinetics
|reaction in which the rate is directly proportional to the concentration of a single reactant

|fission
|the process of splitting a heavy atomic nucleus, such as that of uranium or plutonium, into two or more smaller nuclei, releasing a large amount of energy

|FP
|f-factor in physics, a conversion between radiation exposure (roengens) to the absorbed dose (rads)

|fractionation
|the process of separating a mixture of isotopes or radionuclides into individual components

|fusion
|the process of combining atomic nuclei to form a heavier nucleus, releasing a massive amount of energy

|Geiger-Müller
|portable instrument used to detect and measure ionizing radiation, also known as "Geiger counter"

|geostrophic
|the balance between the coriolis force and the horizontal pressure force in the atmosphere

|half life (t 1/2)
|time required for a quantity of a substance to reduce to half of its initial value through a process of exponential decay

|heptasulfide
|chemical compound containing seven sulfur atoms in each molecule

|heptoxide
|chemical compound containing seven oxygen atoms in each molecule

|inert
|unmoving, stopped

|ingrowth emitter
|a radioactive isotope that produces additional radioactive materials as it decays

|insoluble
|incapable of being dissolved by a substance

|inversion layer
|where a layer of warm air is situated above cooler air in the atmosphere

|ionization
|process where an atom loses or gains electrons, becoming a charged ion, typically as a result of interactions with high-energy nuclear radiation like alpha or beta particles

|IPC filter
|air filter made by IPC Eagle (a brand of Idrobase Group)

|isoplething
|plotting a map which connects points of equal value for a specific variable, specifically atmospheric dispersion in this case

|isotope
|two or more forms of the same element with equal numbers of protons each but varying numbers of electrons

|Ivy Mike test
|the codename for the first-full scale test of a thermonuclear device, part of a series in Operation Ivy

|KeV time
|measurement of time that descibes the neutron fluence (time-integrated flux), measured in moles

|KSCM compounds (KSCM)
|synthesized benzofuran compounds, dpm/kscm = disintegrations per 1000 standard cubic meters (dpm/kscm)

|Lagrangian
|tracks the motion of individual particles, measuring their position, velocity, and acceleration overtime by following them through a fluid

|Liquid Metal Breeder Reactor (LMBR)
|nuclear reactor that uses a liquid metal (such as liquid sodium) as the primary coolant and is designed to "breed" more fissile material than it consumes

|low background
|experiments or detectors designed to have an extremely low level of background radiation to detect rare nuclear events

|mass number
|the mass number _A_ of an atom is the total number of protons and neutrons in a nucleus (_^A^element_)

|megaton (MT)
|one million tons

|megawatt day exposure (mwd)
|a unit of energy to measure fuel burn-up

|mole
|measurement of the number of atoms, molecules or ions (1 mole = Avogadro's number, = 6.022x10^23^)

|most probable charge (Zp)
|the most likely proton number of a fission fragment within an isobaric chain in nuclear fission

|nanogram (ng)
|1 billionth of a gram

|neutron capture
|nuclear reaction where a nucleus absorbs a neutron, resulting in a heavier nucleus or a new element

|neutron flux
|Φ, measurement of intensity of neutron radiation

|nitric acid
|corrosive, poisonous with strong oxidizing properties, made in a laboratory by distilling nitrates and sulfuric acid

|nuclear reactor
|a device that houses and controls a sustained nuclear chain reaction, most commonly through fission, to produce a steady and controllable release of energy

|nuclide
|isotope

|oceanographic
|the physical and biological properties and phenomena of the sea

|perchclorate (ClO [.overline]#~4~#)
|compounds containing the perchlorate anion

|perrhenate (ReO [.overline]#~4~#)
|compounds containing the perrhenate anion

|plume
|gas or aerosol released into the atmosphere at an approximately steady rate

|production rate
|number of specific nuclei, particles, or radioactive isotopes created per unit of time and per unit of volume

|proportional counter
|a gas-filled ionization detector in nuclear physics that measures the energy of ionizing radiation

|radioactive decay
|the process where an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation

|radioactive yield
|percentage of a radioactive product's activity compared to the starting radioactivity, both decay-corrected to the same point in time

|radiotoxicity
|percentage of a radioactive product's activity compared to the starting radioactivity, both decay-corrected to the same point in time

|radionuclides
|unstable forms of chemical element that releases radiation

|rads
|unit of measurement for absorbed dose of ionizing radiation, corresponding to the absorbption of 0.01 Joule/kg

|roentgens
|legacy (inactive) unit of measurement for x-rays and gamma rays

|scm
|standard cubic meter, a unit of volume for a gas measured under standard conditions of temperature and pressure

|Sf
|spontaneous fission

|spallation reaction
|nuclear reaction where a heavy nucleus, struck by a high-energy particle, breaks apart and ejects many fragments, including neutrons, protons, and lighter nuclei

|substrate
|base layer or material on which a nuclear target is prepared or deposited

|tetraphenyl arsonium chloride
|organoarsenic compound used as a reagent in organic synthesis and analytical chemistry

|tracer
|substances added into a system which can be used to track movement and behavior of physical, chemical, or biological processes

|transuranic
|has a higher atomic number than uranium (92)

|tropopause
|the boundary layer between the troposphere and the stratosphere, located at an altitude of roughly 8 km at the poles and 18 km at the equator

|σ
|lowercase sigma (Greek), standard deviation

|∑
|uppercase sigma (Greek); sum

|θ
|lowercase theta (Greek)

|ū
|denotes an average value, a mean, or specific variable in a given model

|σθū
|covariance between the variable θ and a defined variable _u_

|μm
|unit of measurement of length, equal to one millionth of a meter (micrometer)

|===
